Guide to Spotting Fake News

What is Fake News?

Fake news refers to false or misleading information presented as news,
including fabricated stories, altered images, and deceptive headlines
designed to manipulate public opinion.

Types of Fake News

  • Misinformation: Incorrect info shared without intent to deceive.
  • Disinformation: Deliberately false info meant to mislead.
  • Malinformation: Real information used maliciously to harm.

How to Spot Fake News

  • Check the source’s credibility.
  • Read beyond the headline to get the full context.
  • Look for supporting evidence from multiple sources.
  • Evaluate the author’s expertise.
  • Watch for emotional or biased language.

Tools to Detect Fake News

  • Use fact-checking websites (e.g., Snopes, PolitiFact).
  • Use Google Reverse Image Search to verify images.
  • Cross-reference stories from multiple sources.
  • Explore browser extensions or AI tools for misinformation detection.

Tips for Responsible Sharing

  • Verify information before sharing.
  • Educate others about identifying fake news.
  • Report misinformation on social media platforms.
